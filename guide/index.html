CHAPTER ZERO

THE LEGEND OF ELDORIA

Long, long ago — before the meadow paths were walked, before the first lantern was lit — the land of Eldoria was wild and dark. It was the Crown of Eldoria, forged by the first heroes from three sacred jewels, that brought the balance: the Emerald of the deep forests, the Ruby of the burning sands, and the Sapphire of the frozen peaks. While the Crown sat whole upon its pedestal, crops grew tall, monsters kept to the shadows, and kids could chase fireflies long after bedtime.

Then came Morgrath.

Once the royal court's cleverest sorcerer, Morgrath grew jealous of a power he could study but never own. One starless night he split the Crown with a single forbidden word, scattering its three shards to temples at the far corners of the realm, each guarded by a monstrous general of his own twisted making. Then he raised the Dark Citadel in the Ashen Wastes and sealed himself behind a barrier no blade, bomb, or battering ram can break.

The land dimmed. Slimes oozed into the meadows. Skeletons climbed out of the desert. Wolves of living ice came down from the mountains. And in the little village of Willow, the elder Rowan looked at one brave kid — you — and saw the only hope Eldoria had left.

TALES OF ELDORIA "Three shards, three temples, three generals. Reunite the shards and the barrier falls. Then it is you and Morgrath... and may your sword arm be steady, little one." — Elder Rowan

SAVING YOUR QUEST

Three save slots live on the title screen. The game auto-saves every time you enter or leave an area, find a treasure, or defeat a boss — and you can save anytime: press ENTER, then S. Falling in battle never erases your progress. Continue from your last save and march right back in!

WATCH OUT! Pressing E twice on the title screen ERASES a save slot forever. Guard your slot like you guard your hearts!

READING YOUR HUD

Top of the screen, left to right: your hearts (lose them all and it's Game Over), gems, arrows and bombs (once you own their launchers), potions, dungeon keys, your three shard slots, and the item bound to X in the far corner box.

FIRST THINGS FIRST

  • Visit Elder Rowan in the big house — he hands every new hero 30 gems to get started. Free money!
  • Cut tall grass and bushes with your sword. Hearts, gems and arrows hide inside. Smash pots too — it's not rude, it's adventuring.
  • Talk to everyone twice. Villagers update their hints as your quest moves forward.

HP = sword hits to defeat (Hero's Blade halves it!). DMG = hearts lost on contact.

Spicier cousin. Hops with less downtime — strike between bounces.

A rude plant. Sidestep its seeds and rush it — it barely moves.

WALKTHROUGH — CHAPTER ONE

FROM VILLAGE TO VINEWOOD

Step one: see Elder Rowan (30 free gems!), read every sign, and mow the village lawns for pocket change. When your courage is topped up, take the west road out of the village square.

Step two: the crossroads. South lie the farms; keep west through the meadows. Slimes and the odd red slime patrol here — perfect sword practice. Work toward the forest border two screens west.

Step three: the forest. Tall trees, winding paths, spitters lobbing seeds from the undergrowth, and bats in the canopy. Head for the north-west clearing where stone statues flank a mossy staircase: Vinewood Temple.

VINEWOOD TEMPLE — down the stairs and into Dungeon 1!
PRO TIP Before the temple: stock up! 20 gems buys 10 arrows — useless now (no bow yet), but a potion (40) in your pocket turns a scary first dungeon into a comfy one. Short on gems? There's a 20-gem chest behind bushes in the far north-west forest nook, and a 50-gem chest in the south-west woods.

SIDE QUEST: THE SQUIRREL KING

In the forest's western clearing, a suspicious wall of bushes hides a grove. Cut through and meet NUTWICK, KING OF SQUIRRELS, who pays out for Golden Acorns at 4, 8 and 12 collected. There's an acorn lying in his own grove. Take it. He won't notice. (He will absolutely notice. He counts them daily. Take it anyway.)

THE HIDDEN GROVE — His Royal Fluffiness awaits your tribute.
TALES OF ELDORIA Why are the acorns golden? Morgrath's barrier leaks raw magic on the wind. It lands on ordinary acorns. Nutwick considers this the one good thing the sorcerer ever did.

WALKTHROUGH — CHAPTER TWO

DUNGEON 1: VINEWOOD TEMPLE

FULL TEMPLE MAP — entry at bottom-center. Boss waits top-center!
  1. Entry hall: push the stone block onto the floor plate to open the north door. Your first puzzle — the block slides one tile per shove.
  2. South-west room: clear every monster and a Small Key #1 chest appears. Sealed doors with monsters behind them work like this all game: clear the room, claim the prize.
  3. The hub: note the eye switch staring at you. It wants an arrow you don't have yet. East first!
  4. Locked door (key #1) east: the BIG SLIME miniboss guards a golden big chest — the HUNTING BOW (with 10 arrows). Dodge the hops, strike the landings.
  5. Supply room (north of the miniboss): plates + a fight open the way to spare arrows and gems. Stock up.
  6. Back to the hub: arrow the eye switch — the west door grinds open. Small Key #2 lives in the south-west wing.
  7. North-west vault: the BIG KEY. Now the ominous boss door in the hub's north wall will open.
SECRET The south-east bonus room has a cracked wall. You can't open it yet — remember it, and come back after Dungeon 2 with bombs for a 50-gem payday.

BOSS: BRAMBLEHORN

BRAMBLEHORN — 14 HP of angry beetle. Wait for the WALL CRASH!

The temple's general is a beetle the size of a wagon, and your sword bounces right off its shell. Here's the trick the temple priests carved into the walls: it can't steer. Stand in its line, wait for the telegraph flash, hop aside — and when it slams into the wall, it's stunned and soft for a full three seconds. That's your window. Slash, retreat, repeat. Reward: Heart Container + THE EMERALD SHARD!

PRO TIP Take the glowing portal home — and visit Elder Rowan. Everyone in the village has new things to say after each shard!

WALKTHROUGH — CHAPTER THREE

DUNGEON 2: SANDSTONE RUINS

Getting in: cross the south meadows into the desert and head for the great ruin in the south-east. The door is sealed — but a stone eye watches from the facade. You have a bow now. You know what to do.

ONE ARROW OPENS THE WAY. Zara tried everything else for years.
  1. Entry: go WEST first — a lantern sits in a chest in plain sight. (Already bought one from Mira? The chest pays gems instead.)
  2. East kill room: clear it for Key #1, then light the two torches to unseal the north door.
  3. MUMMY KNIGHT miniboss: bait the dash, punish the recovery. Victory opens the vault — the golden chest holds the BOMB BAG (with 5 bombs).
  4. Key #1 unlocks the entry's north door — the hub. Twin eye switches here open a handy shortcut east.
  5. West wing: push-blocks onto plates plus a torch open the way to the BIG KEY vault. Key #2 is in this wing too, in a spike nook — careful feet!
  6. North wing (dark!): lantern up. A pit maze guards Key #3, and a cracked wall here must be bombed to push deeper.
  7. Gauntlet east, then the boss door. Potion check: got one?
SECRET FOUR more cracked walls hide bonus caches in this dungeon — west of the entry, in the big-key vault, in the gauntlet, and beside the bomb vault. When in doubt, bomb the suspicious wall. The ruins respect demolition.

BOSS: VESPER THE SAND WYRM

20 HP OF BURROWING NIGHTMARE. Only the surfaced wyrm can be hurt!

The arena floor churns. The wyrm is untouchable underground — watch the sand bulge, step away from the rising spot, and dodge the spit. Then it surfaces for five long seconds: attack! Sword if you're brave, arrows if you're sensible. Reward: Heart Container + THE RUBY SHARD!

WATCH OUT! Out of bombs mid-dungeon? Mummies drop them, and Mira sells five for 25. Never bomb your last wall without a plan, hero.

WALKTHROUGH — CHAPTER FOUR

DUNGEON 3: GLACIER HOLLOW

Getting in: take the mountain pass north (Tomas will fret — wave cheerfully). On the high snowfield, a cracked boulder plugs a cave mouth. One bomb later, Glacier Hollow yawns open.

THE BOULDER. Hermit Aldous has been staring at it for 20 years. You brought bombs.

Ice rules: on blue ice you slide — steer gently, plan ahead. Blocks pushed on ice glide until they hit something. Every sokoban puzzle here is built on that. (Mess one up? Leave the room and return — blocks reset. The glacier is forgiving. The wolves are not.)

SOKOBAN ON ICE — line the block up so an ice block stops it ON the plate.
  1. West of entry: the ice-slide maze. Ride it to Key #1.
  2. East of entry: wolf den (clear-sealed) into the sokoban on ice — plates open a pocket holding Key #2.
  3. Hub (locked door #1): a cracked wall in the hub itself hides Key #3. Bombs again!
  4. West wing: triple-plate sokoban, then the sentinel vault — clear the eye sentries for the BIG KEY. Approach sentries diagonally!
  5. East wing: the three flames hall (lantern!) leads to the ALPHA WOLF arena. Let it crash into walls, then strike. The golden chest holds the POWER BOOTS — faster forever, and no more slipping on ice.
  6. North crevasse (optional): pits, sentries, treasure. Boots help.
  7. Hub north: the boss door.

BOSS: KARN THE FROST REVENANT

22 HP, TELEPORTS, SUMMONS. The busiest fight yet — stay moving!

A frozen sorcerer who blinks around the arena hurling fans of ice. Halfway down, he calls frost wisps to the fight — swat them fast or drown in shards. He's vulnerable whenever he's visible: chase the materialize, land two hits, dodge the barrage, repeat. Reward: Heart Container + THE SAPPHIRE SHARD!

WALKTHROUGH — CHAPTER FIVE

THE BARRIER & THE DARK CITADEL

Three shards sing in your pack. East of the mountain pass, the Ashen Wastes begin — dead trees, boiling lava, and Morgrath's shimmering Barrier. Walk up to it with all three shards and watch it dissolve like sugar in rain. No button needed. You earned this.

THE BARRIER FALLS — the Wastes are open. So is the worst place in the world.
WATCH OUT! The Wastes are end-game country: dark knights (ARROW-PROOF!), wizards, skeletons. Lava walls burn on touch. There's a 50-gem cache and a 5-bomb cache hidden out here for heroes who explore — see the Classified Section.

The Dark Citadel looms in the north-east. Checklist before the gates: Hero's Blade (page 16!), 4 potions, full bombs and arrows, and every Heart Container you can claim. This place is a fortress, a puzzle box and a final exam all at once.

THE DARK CITADEL — entry at bottom-center; Morgrath's throne crowns the top.

THE SAFE ROUTE (4 KEYS, ZERO REGRETS)

  1. Key #1: the gauntlet room east of the entry — seal yourself in, clear it out.
  2. Key #2: the crypt west of the entry. Dark room — lantern up.
  3. Key #1 opens the entry's north door — the hub.
  4. Key #3: west of the hub past the sokoban. Key #4: the war room east of the pre-boss hall.
  5. BIG KEY: the lava gallery (north-west) — eye switches across the lava open the path. Don't hug the lava walls; they burn.
  6. Hub north → sanctum → throne room.
PRO TIP Every key in the Citadel can be reached WITHOUT spending one — so you can never lock yourself out. The architect was evil, not cruel.
SECRET The torch room east of the hub hides a bombable alcove — inside is the Citadel's own HEART CONTAINER. Do not skip this.

WALKTHROUGH — FINAL CHAPTER

MORGRATH: THE LAST BATTLE

PHASE ONE — THE SORCERER

PHASE 1 — the wizard duel. He blinks, he barrages, he gloats.

Morgrath fights like his wizards, only meaner: teleport, barrage, rest, repeat. The rhythm is your friend. When he materializes, he needs a moment to wind up — that moment belongs to your blade. Two clean hits per window, then break away before the bolts fan out. Keep your hearts above half: phase one is a warm-up, and you want to enter what's next strong.

PHASE TWO — THE DEMON

HE WASN'T KIDDING ABOUT THE FORBIDDEN MAGIC. 26 HP of doom.

Drop the sorcerer and the throne room shakes... and what stands up is no longer a man. Demon Morgrath fills the room: fireball fans, charging rushes that crack the floor, and bat swarms when his health runs low. The plan:

  • Charges: he crashes like Bramblehorn — sidestep and slash the recovery. Old tricks beat new demons.
  • Fireball fans: the gaps are generous. Walk — don't panic-run — through them. Boots help.
  • Bats: kill them THE SECOND they appear, or they'll nickel-and-dime your potion stash away.
  • Potions: drink at 2 hearts. A held potion heals zero damage.

When the last hit lands, the darkness peels away, the Crown reforges, and Eldoria gets its sky back. You did that. Roll credits, hero — and yes, your save keeps going afterward, with every secret still out there to find.

THE SUNKEN SHRINE & THE HERO'S BLADE

Witch Morla's riddle is real. South-west through the swamp — wade the shallows, mind the deep water — ancient stones rise from the mire. The staircase between them sinks into the SUNKEN SHRINE, the one dungeon no quest requires... and the one every hero should finish.

OLD STONES DREAM BELOW THE MIRE. Bring your lantern — only its light wakes them.
ALL FOUR ROOMS — small dungeon, giant reward.
  1. Antechamber: block on the plate AND both torches lit (lantern!) opens the north door.
  2. Drowned cellar (east, optional): clear the fight for 50 gems.
  3. Hall of echoing lights: two block-plates, one torch. The east door grinds open.
  4. The arena: BOG LURKER. Swing where it surfaces. Its treasure: the HERO'S BLADE — double damage on every swing, forever.

MORE CLASSIFIED INTEL

  • Fairy ponds (lake NE + desert oasis): walk into the water, leave with full hearts. Free. Reusable. The pros plan routes around them.
  • Gem caves: bomb-boulders in the central meadows and SE forest each hide a 50-gem chest (the forest one has acorn #5!).
  • Wastes caches: 50 gems in the NW ruin pocket; FIVE bombs in the NE — restock before the Citadel without walking home.
  • Chest patrol: 20-gem chests hide in the meadow bluffs, the south hollow, the forest nook, the lake cove, the peaks and the swamp; 50-gem chests in the SW forest and east desert; an arrow cache sits in the north desert. Our world map shows the regions — happy hunting!
  • Dungeon 1 revisit: that cracked wall in Vinewood's SE room? 50 gems say you came back with bombs.
